## 📌 Slide 2 — What is Intelligence?

**Key Definition (Wikipedia):**

> Intelligence is the capacity for **abstraction, logic, understanding, self-awareness, learning, emotional knowledge, reasoning, planning, creativity, critical thinking**, and **problem-solving**.
> It is the ability to **perceive or infer information**, and to **retain it as knowledge** to be applied to **adaptive behaviors** within an environment or context.

### Breaking it down — What does Intelligence include?

| Ability             | Simple Meaning                                      |
| ------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| Abstraction         | Thinking about ideas beyond the concrete world      |
| Logic               | Reasoning in a structured, consistent way           |
| Understanding       | Grasping meaning, not just data                     |
| Self-awareness      | Knowing that you exist and can reflect on yourself  |
| Learning            | Gaining new knowledge from experience               |
| Emotional knowledge | Understanding feelings — yours and others'          |
| Reasoning           | Drawing conclusions from information                |
| Planning            | Thinking ahead about future actions                 |
| Creativity          | Generating new ideas and solutions                  |
| Critical thinking   | Evaluating information carefully and questioning it |
| Problem-solving     | Finding solutions to challenges                     |

### 💡 Key Insight

Intelligence is NOT just one thing — it is a **collection of overlapping abilities** that together allow an entity (human or machine) to **survive and thrive in its environment**.

---

## 📌 Slide 3 — DIKW Pyramid

### The DIKW Pyramid: From Raw Data → Wisdom

```
        🔺 WISDOM       ← highest: knowing WHEN and WHY to act
       🔺🔺 KNOWLEDGE   ← understanding patterns and meaning
      🔺🔺🔺 INFORMATION ← data with context and relevance
     🔺🔺🔺🔺 DATA       ← raw, unprocessed facts and figures
```

### Each Level Explained

**1. DATA (Bottom)**

- Raw, unorganized facts — numbers, symbols, events
- By itself, has no meaning
- Example: `37, Berlin, Thursday, 15:00`

**2. INFORMATION**

- Data + Context = meaningful
- Example: `Temperature in Berlin on Thursday at 3pm is 37°C`
- Now it means something!

**3. KNOWLEDGE**

- Information + Experience/Patterns
- Example: `37°C is extremely hot for Berlin — heatwave conditions likely`
- You understand the _implication_

**4. WISDOM (Top)**

- Knowledge + Judgment → best action
- Example: `Cancel outdoor events, warn vulnerable people, increase water supply`
- You know WHAT to do and WHY

### 🔑 Why DIKW matters for AI

AI systems are built to climb this pyramid — from processing raw **data** → extracting **information** → building **knowledge** → eventually making **wise decisions** autonomously.

## 📌 Slide 4 — What is Artificial Intelligence?

**Key Definition (Wikipedia):**

> **Artificial Intelligence (AI)**, in its broadest sense, is intelligence exhibited by **machines**, particularly **computer systems**. It is a field of research that develops methods and software that enable machines to:
>
> - **Perceive their environment**
> - **Use learning and intelligence**
> - **Take actions** that maximize their chances of achieving defined goals

### Human Intelligence vs. Artificial Intelligence

| Feature        | Human Intelligence                | Artificial Intelligence           |
| -------------- | --------------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Learning       | From experience, school, emotions | From data, training sets          |
| Speed          | Slower                            | Much faster (at specific tasks)   |
| Adaptability   | Broad, flexible                   | Narrow (usually specific domains) |
| Self-awareness | Yes                               | No (currently)                    |
| Creativity     | High                              | Emerging                          |
| Fatigue        | Yes                               | No                                |

### 💡 Key Insight

AI does NOT need to be exactly like human intelligence — it just needs to **perceive, learn, and act** in a way that achieves goals. This is why a chess-playing computer IS AI even without emotions.

## 📌 Slide 5 — What is an Agent?

### The Agent Diagram (very important!)

```
ENVIRONMENT ←──────────────────────────────┐
     │                                      │
     │ (percepts via sensors)               │
     ▼                                      │
  AGENT 🤖                                  │
  [processes info, decides]                 │
     │                                      │
     │ (actions via actuators)              │
     ▼                                      │
ENVIRONMENT ───────────────────────────────►│
```

### The 4 Key Components of an Agent

| Component     | What it does                           | Human Equivalent           |
| ------------- | -------------------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| **Sensors**   | Perceive the environment (input)       | Eyes, ears, nose           |
| **Percepts**  | The information received from sensors  | What you see/hear          |
| **Actuators** | Physical mechanism to act              | Hands, legs, mouth         |
| **Actions**   | What the agent does in the environment | Walking, talking, clicking |

### 💡 Simple Example: A Self-Driving Car as an Agent

- **Sensors** → cameras, radar, GPS
- **Percepts** → detects a red light ahead
- **Processing** → "I must stop"
- **Actuators** → brakes
- **Action** → car stops

## 📌 Slide 6 — What is Agency?

**Key Definition (Wikipedia):**

> **Agency** is the **capacity of an actor to act** in a given environment.

### What makes Agency special?

Agency = the **ability to make choices and carry them out** — not just being pushed by external forces.

| WITHOUT Agency                   | WITH Agency                                         |
| -------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| A rock rolling downhill          | A person choosing to walk downhill                  |
| A thermostat reacting to heat    | A smart home AI _deciding_ the best temperature     |
| A robot following a fixed script | An AI agent adapting its strategy to new situations |

### 💡 Key Insight

Agency implies **autonomy** — the ability to decide and act based on internal goals, not just external triggers.

---

## 📌 Slide 7 — What is Agentic AI?

**Key Definition (Wikipedia):**

> **Agentic AI** is a class of artificial intelligence that focuses on **autonomous systems** that can **make decisions and perform tasks with limited or no human intervention**.

### Regular AI vs. Agentic AI

| Feature      | Regular AI                       | Agentic AI                                                              |
| ------------ | -------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Input/Output | You give input → it gives output | It perceives environment on its own                                     |
| Human role   | Always needed to trigger         | Minimal or none                                                         |
| Planning     | No long-term planning            | Plans multiple steps ahead                                              |
| Goal pursuit | Answers one question             | Pursues a goal over time                                                |
| Example      | ChatGPT answering a question     | An AI agent that books your flights, hotel, and trip plan automatically |

### 🔑 The 3 Powers of Agentic AI

1. **Perception** — senses its environment
2. **Decision-making** — chooses the best action autonomously
3. **Action** — executes the decision without needing human approval every step

## 📌 Slides 8–9 — Vannevar Bush (1890–1974) & "As We May Think" (1945)

### Who was Vannevar Bush?

- American engineer, inventor, science administrator
- During WWII, led the U.S. **Office of Scientific Research and Development (OSRD)**
- Oversaw radar development and early **Manhattan Project**
- Founded the company that became **Raytheon**
- Became VP of MIT and Dean of MIT School of Engineering

### Why does he matter for AI?

In **1945**, he wrote a famous essay: **"As We May Think"** published in _The Atlantic_

### Key Ideas from "As We May Think"

> Machines have extended human **physical** powers (hammers, microscopes). Now we need machines that extend human **mental** powers.

Bush argued that:

- There is a **growing mountain of knowledge** that humans cannot handle alone
- Scientists need tools to **store, link, and retrieve** information the way the human mind does — by **association**, not alphabetical index
- He called for a new relationship between **thinking humans and the sum of knowledge**

### 💡 Historical Significance

This 1945 essay is considered one of the **founding visions of the internet and AI** — written decades before computers existed as we know them.

---

## 📌 Slides 10–11 — The Memex (Memory Index)

### What was Memex?

A **hypothetical device** imagined by Vannevar Bush — essentially a **personal knowledge machine**

### How Memex worked (concept):

- Stored all of a person's books, records, and communications on **microfilm**
- User could **link** related pieces of information (like hyperlinks today!)
- Could be **shared** with others — trails of associated knowledge
- Could **annotate** documents with personal notes

### Memex's Vision for Different Professionals

| Professional    | How Memex would help                                   |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| Lawyer          | Access all associated opinions and decisions instantly |
| Patent Attorney | Call up millions of patents with related trails        |
| Doctor          | Run through analogous case histories rapidly           |
| Historian       | Follow trails across civilizations and epochs          |

### 🔑 Why Memex = Early AI/Internet Vision

The Memex concept introduced the idea of:

- **Hypertext** (linked information)
- **Personal knowledge bases**
- **Associative memory** (the way AI memory works today)
- **Collaborative knowledge sharing**

> **Memex → Web → Search Engines → AI Knowledge Systems** — it's all connected.

## 📌 Slides 12–13 — Douglas Engelbart & "The Mother of All Demos" (1963–1968)

### Douglas Engelbart's Paper (1963)

**"A Conceptual Framework for the Augmentation of Man's Intellect"**  
— Stanford Research Institute

Key idea: **Augmenting human intellect** = increasing the ability of humans to approach complex problems, gain comprehension, and derive solutions.

This means:

- Comprehension can be gained **more quickly**
- Problems **too complex** before can now be solved
- Solutions can be found where **none existed** before

### The Mother of All Demos (December 9, 1968)

A landmark 90-minute live computer demonstration by **Douglas Engelbart** at ACM/IEEE in San Francisco.

**First time EVER publicly demonstrated (all in one system):**

| Technology Demonstrated         | Still used today?     |
| ------------------------------- | --------------------- |
| Windows (graphical windows)     | ✅ Yes                |
| Hypertext (clickable links)     | ✅ Yes                |
| Graphics on screen              | ✅ Yes                |
| Video conferencing              | ✅ Yes                |
| Computer mouse                  | ✅ Yes                |
| Word processing                 | ✅ Yes                |
| Dynamic file linking            | ✅ Yes                |
| Revision control                | ✅ Yes                |
| Collaborative real-time editing | ✅ Yes (Google Docs!) |

### 🔑 Impact

- Directly influenced **Xerox PARC** in the 1970s
- Led to **Apple Macintosh** and **Microsoft Windows**
- The entire modern computing experience traces back to this one demo

```
1890 ──── Vannevar Bush born

1968 ──── "Mother of All Demos" — mouse, hypertext, video conferencing

1991 ──── World Wide Web — Memex vision finally realized
2000s ─── Search engines, Wikipedia — knowledge linking at scale
2020s ─── Agentic AI — machines now perceive, decide, and act autonomously
```

## Intelligence kya hai?

Intelligence (Buddhi) ke bahut saare matlab hain. Simple bhasha mein — yeh woh shakti hai jisse hum:

Sochte hain (logic, reasoning)
Seekhte hain (learning)
Naye ideas banate hain (creativity)
Mushkilon ko solve karte hain (problem-solving)
Khud ko samajhte hain (self-awareness)
Apne aas-paas ke hisaab se khud ko badal lete hain (adaptive behavior)

Ek line mein: Intelligence = apne environment ko samajhna aur usme sahi action lena.

## DIKW Pyramid (Bahut Important!)

Yeh ek pyramid hai jo dikhata hai ki raw information kaise wisdom banti hai:
🔺 WISDOM — sahi waqt par sahi faisla karna
🔺 KNOWLEDGE — patterns aur matlab samajhna
🔺 INFORMATION — data jo meaningful ho
🔺 DATA — kaccha, seedha number ya fact
Real Example:

Data: 37°C, Berlin, Thursday
Information: Berlin mein Thursday ko 37°C temperature hai
Knowledge: Yeh Berlin ke liye bahut zyada garmi hai — heatwave hai
Wisdom: Outdoor programs cancel karo, paani ki supply badhao, budhon ko alert karo

AI ke liye kyon zaroori hai? AI bhi yahi karta hai — pehle raw data leta hai, phir usse information, phir knowledge, phir wise decision banana seekhta hai.

## Artificial Intelligence kya hai?

AI = Machines dwara dikhaya gaya intelligence — khaas kar computer systems mein.
AI ka kaam hai:

Apne environment ko perceive karna (dekhna/sunna)
Seekhna — data se patterns nikalna
Action lena — apne goal ki taraf badhna

Insaan vs AI:

Insaan bahut cheezein seekh sakta hai, emotions samajhta hai
AI ek specific kaam mein bahut fast aur accurate hota hai
AI thakta nahi, lekin abhi tak "self-aware" nahi hai

## Agent kya hota hai?

Agent = koi bhi entity jo apne environment ko sense kare aur action le.
4 parts hote hain ek agent ke:
PartKya karta haiInsaan mein

- Sensors Environment ko feel karna Aankhein, kaan
- Percepts Jo information sensors ne li Jo tumne dekha/suna
- Actuators Action karne ka zariya Haath, pair
- Actions Jo actually kiya Chalna, bolna
  Example — Self-Driving Car:

Sensor → Camera ne red light dekhi
Percept → "Red light hai aage"
Decision → "Rukna chahiye"
Actuator → Brakes
Action → Car ruk gayi ✅

## Agency kya hai?

Agency = Khud se kuch karne ki kshamta (capacity to act on your own).
Simple fark:

Patthar pahaad se girna = Agency nahi (koi choice nahi)
Insaan ka pahaad se neeche jaana = Agency hai (choice thi)

AI mein agency ka matlab hai — machine apne aap decide karti hai aur kaam karti hai, koi push nahi chahiye.

---

## Agentic AI kya hai?

Agentic AI = Woh AI jo khud se decisions leta hai aur tasks complete karta hai — bina ya bahut kam insani madad ke.
Regular AI vs Agentic AI:

Regular AI → Aap poochho → woh jawab de
Agentic AI → Aap ek goal do → woh khud plan banaye, steps le, kaam poora kare

Example:

Regular AI: "Mumbai ka weather batao" → jawab milta hai
Agentic AI: "Meri Mumbai trip plan karo" → flight dhundhe, hotel book kare, itinerary banaye — sab khud!

## Vannevar Bush aur "As We May Think" (1945)

Vannevar Bush ek American scientist the jo WWII ke dauran US government ke liye scientific research head karte the (radar, Manhattan Project).
1945 mein unhone ek article likha: "As We May Think"
Unka bada sawaal tha: Machines ne insaan ki body ki shakti badha di (hammers, microscopes) — ab machines insaan ki dimag ki shakti kyon nahi badha sakti?
Unka vision:

Bahut zyada knowledge hai duniya mein — koi akela insaan sab nahi samjh sakta
Ek aisa machine chahiye jo information ko store kare, link kare, jaldi dhundhe
Jaise insaan ka dimag — association se sochta hai (ek cheez → doosri yaad aana)

Yeh article aaj bhi AI aur Internet ka founding vision mana jata hai — 1945 mein likha gaya, jab computers bhi nahi the!

## Memex (Memory Index)

Bush ne ek hypothetical machine imagine ki — Memex
Kya karta Memex:

Ek person ki saari books, notes, research microfilm pe store karta
Documents ko link karta — jaise aaj hum hyperlinks karte hain
Notes likh sakte the documents pe
Yeh "trails" doosron ke saath share kar sakte the

Aaj se compare karein:

Memex links → Internet hyperlinks
Memex storage → Cloud storage
Memex trails → Browser bookmarks + Google
Memex sharing → Wikipedia, shared documents

Bush ne 1945 mein internet ka sapna dekha tha — bina internet ke!

1945 mein Bush ne sapna dekha → 1968 mein Engelbart ne dikhaya → 1990s mein Internet bana → 2020s mein Agentic AI aa gayi
